TEXAS ECONOMY

Census Data Shows Household Earning Disparities In Texas Cities

By Hiram Alejandro Duran

Texans often hear sensationalized accounts about the splendors our capital brings. Austin, the live music capital of the world, might have a stronger draw than its lauded cultural scene. According to data retrieved from the U.S. Census Bureau, Austin has the largest percentage of households earning above $50,000 across Texas largest cities.

Approximately, 58.60 percent of Austin households earn above the aforementioned earnings-margin. Data shows, the average Austin household earns $60,939 in earnings per year surpassing the national average of $55,322. The largest cluster of Austin residents above the aforementioned household-income margin, 17.80 percent, earn between $50,000 and $74,999.

Meanwhile, El Paso, the smallest of the cities considered in this analysis shows a substantially lower margin of residents earning above $50,000, 43.40 percent. The average El Paso household earns a yearly average of $43,322. Only two Texan metropolitan areas, Austin and Fort Worth, have more than half of their residents earning above $50,000.
